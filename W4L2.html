Introduction

Karthikeyan’s article is proposing a new model, using a neural network to analyze compounds and predict the melting points of said compounds. What I had to do was analyze3e the data in order to find correlation and causality between 2D factors, and the compounds melting point. After finding basic correlations with pairwise plots, and regression plots, I was able to perform BIC analyses to find causation if there was any. I found that the 2D descriptors that I measured heavily affected the melting point with differences in BIC scores >100. The article uses p scores to find weather or not signs were statistical significant, whereas I can look at the BIC score. Overall it is interesting to see the different methods used between this deliverable and the article, and the fact that they can achieve similar results. ## Importing data and Library For this project I am using tidyverse, dyplr and reading melting point data from M. Karthikeyan.

mp$energy <- ifelse(is.na(mp$energy), mean(mp$energy, na.rm=TRUE), mp$energy)
mp$dipole.moment[mp$dipole.moment == 0] <- NA
mp$dipole.moment <- ifelse(is.na(mp$energy), mean(mp$energy, na.rm = TRUE), mp$energy)
